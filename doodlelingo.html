Doodle Lingo

Summary

Doodle Lingo is an educational app that provides fun ways for children to learn English. By combining drawing with language learning, children visually understand and memorize the language more easily. Children will create drawings that they can play with while practicing new vocabulary and sentences.

Overview

My son was born in Korea, but we moved to the U.S. when he was 11 months old. We also lived in Germany for two years. From this experience, I learned how challenging it is to learn a second and third language. That motivated me to create my final thesis project around this challenge.

Problems

Keeping young learners engaged and interested is a challenge. Because...

  • Text-based workbooks do not work as well for young children, because they have not yet developed their reading skills.
  • Monotonous programs can be boring for some learners, causing them to quickly lose interest in the learning material.
  • Feeling frustrated Educational apps can be difficult to understand for young children how to control because of their limited cognitive and physical abilities.
How can we help children to learn English in an easy and enjoyable way?

Solutions

To achieve the goal;
First, the app provides the drawing feature to stimulate curiosity before learning a new vocabulary.
Second, the app provides an interactive animation to help users memorize content in an enjoyable way.
Lastly, the app provides coach marks and audio guidelines to aid users in the app interface.

Research

Why is Drawing a Great Tool for Learning English?

  • Creating a more cohesive memory trace through stimulating neurons and creating more brain connections between them
  • Helping children learn by connecting new and unfamiliar concepts with existing ones
  • Stimulating curiosity before learning a new vocabulary
  • Motivating children to learn

Designing for Kids: Touch Gestures

For designing an app for young children, I needed to learn their physical abilities. Children under 5 have limited motor skills and require very simple physical interactions on touchscreens. In addition, children between 6-8 years old can perform simple trackpad and keyboard usage but is still constrained. Based on the research, I decided to create touchscreen designs emphasizing swiping, tapping, and dragging which are the simplest and most appropriate gestures for young users.

Liu,F. (2018) Design for Kids Based on Their Stage of Physical Development. Nielsen Norman Group.

Designing for Kids: Cognitive, Language, and Fine Motor Skills Development

For defining my target audience, I needed to better understand child development. Based on the common development milestones, 4- to 5-year-old children Know how to sort and classify object according to more complex characteristics and put together four to five words into a sentence. At this age, children can draw geometric shapes and tracing on a line with control. Because of this reason, the minimum age of the target audience is 4, because at the age of 4, children have adequate cognitive abilities and fine motor skills to use mobile apps.
From the target audience interview, I learned that from the ages of 4-7, children are much more familiar with visuals than text, so Doodle Lingo can be a great English learning tool for them.

Target Audience

My primary target audience is children ages 4 to 7 years old, who have limited motor skills and prefer drawing to writing. The Doodle Lingo app would help those children improve their motor skills as well as their English abilities through drawing and various interactive activities that the app provides.
The secondary target audiences are their parents and English teachers.

User Surveys

Survey participants: Nine parents, a UX expert, and an English educator
Date: Oct 12, 2020 - Oct 16, 2020

For user surveys and interviews, I met with children, parents, educators, and UX experts and was able to gather key findings to help guide my project. Over 81% people had experiences to use language learning apps. Also, commonly used gestures are “click” and “drag and drop” so, I incorporated both into Doodle Lingo, not only because they are the most common, but also the most conducive to young children. Target audiences required “easy to use interfaces” and “new and interesting graphics” for children’s apps. I planned to focus on creating simple interfaces and cheerful characters and game screens to make the app useful and kid friendly.The most popular topics for children were vehicles and animals. I decided to include these in the main topic of the app.

User Interviews

Survey participants: Four parents, seven children, a UX expert, and an English educator.
Date: Fall 2020

Through the user interviews, I understood my test subject's confusions and interests.
Yoonchae lives in Korea, and uses language learning apps to help her learn Korean and English. However, sometimes she gets confused with the logistics of the app, how to go to the next step, or navigate its functions. Seungwoo enjoys playing games with various apps. Especially, he likes to customize his characters and animate them to resemble real life. Sehun is always speaking Korean with his family, so speaking English with his friends at school is a challenging switch. He needs to learn English in a fun and effective way to fit in with his friends at school.

Inspirational Projects

I was inspired by these two apps. My son like drawing pictures, so we have been using Bini Bambini a lot, which is an interactive drawing app. He also likes the Todo Math app that provides interactive games to learn math. My son really wanted to find an app like Todo Math for English.
From these experiences, I thought that an app that combines drawing and interactive games could make learning the language more effective and fun.

Competitive Analysis

By analyzing the competitors' pros and cons, I decided to focus on providing various topics for its learning content but keeping the interfaces simple and easy. In addition, Doodle Lingo will provide the coach marks so that children can understand what to do easily. I found that this is one of the shortcomings of other apps.

Sketches, Wireframes, & Prototypes

Sketches

At the beginning of this project, I sketched out my ideas to visually present them to others. At this time, I focused on creating layouts for each page and thinking about how the content would flow for performing each task. I utilized the three task flows to help my logic, and it was a great way to begin thinking about the functionality and design of Doodle Lingo.

Wireframes

I created hand-drawn sketches of each page and quickly realized that paper wireframes would not be able to fully demonstrate the mobile interfaces and interactions. For this reason, I created low-fidelity wireframe using digital prototyping tools for user tests. The first tool I used was Adobe XD, which is simple, easy to use, and my most familiar tool at the time. I created wireframes and printed them out as paper prototypes for the first round of user tests. They were ultimately very useful for understanding the pay layouts and the interface design but were not sufficient to demonstrate interaction design.

Figma is my favorite design tool because it is all-in-one and encapsulates everything from design to prototyping. I used Figma to create high-fidelity mockups and prototypes and used Illustration for detailed graphics on the game background and characters. Digital prototypes using Figma were very helpful for demonstrating not only the interface design but also interaction designs, so I opted for this in user testing.

Testing Process

User Testing

Total 6 rounds of User testing with 13 Participants. .

Results of User Testing

1. Pronounciation Button

Added a microphone button to help users practice pronunciation when they are ready to repeat instead of a three-second countdown.

2. Pronunciation Sound for Quizzes

In order to learn the correct pronunciation, it would be more helpful to provide an expert's voice instead of replaying the user’s voice that was recorded on the pronunciation page for the quiz question.

3. Visual Guide and Coach Mark

To help users understand the direction more easily, provide an animated arrow in a big size instead of a static arrow.

4. Pen Tool and Color Palette (A/B test)

75% of people thought that type B is better. Since the palette goes away, type B could be less distracting for children as they try to draw and learn the word.

5. Various Quiz Games

Doodle Lingo provides content for listening and speaking in the “Draw” feature. So, I added games that help users’ reading and writing in the “Quiz” feature.
In addition, Doodle Lingo will give the user an opportunity to review the quiz game in order to improve their memory.

Technical Process

Technical Project Description Document
Building the Topic Selection Page
  • Used Flickity library to create a carousel slider for the topic selection.
  • Used PHP to query the database based on the topic.
  • Stimulate neurons and create more brain connections between themUsed jQuery to dynamically show the list of drawings based on the topic selected on the carousel slider.
Building the Drawing Page
  • Created a sketchpad with HTML5 Canvas.
  • Created the pen tool button to allow users to unfold the pen tools. (When a user clicks the pen tool button, the pen tool unfolds and the image on the button is changed to the X button, and vice versa.)
  • Used PHP to retrieve the background using a URL parameter.
  • Added three buttons in the fold-up slider to allow the user to change the width of the stroke. (I used JavaScript to change the line width when a user selects the width.)
  • Used the clearRect() function to clear the canvas, then it calls draw() to redraw the background.
  • Created the image save feature to save users’ drawings in the database.

Conclusion

Since this project is for children, understanding target audiences’ needs through target audience interviews were critical to improving the project concept targeted toward children. Designing quiz games to make them not only enjoyable but also helpful in terms of educational value was a challenge for me. However, many rounds of iterative user testing helped me improve interaction and interface design. Through user testing, I found out that this project concept helped children engage in learning English actively and enjoyably. I look forward to publishing the Doodle Lingo App to online mobile app stores, so people can really download and play it in the future.
